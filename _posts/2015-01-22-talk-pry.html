Almost a year ago, I gave a talk at a Ruby Paris meetup about Pry. Pry describes itself as a powerful alternative to the standard IRB shell for Ruby, featuring syntax highlighting, a flexible plugin architecture, runtime invocation and source and documentation browsing.

Pry has been part of my toolbelt for years now, and I cannot conceive developing anything in ruby without it. It comes with lots of stuff making development easier: shell integration, objects/classes inspection, quick access to docs and source code, among others.

I highly recommand checking it out if you are developing anything in ruby, wether a web app or a CLI tool. Pry's wiki is a very good resource to know what it can do and how.
